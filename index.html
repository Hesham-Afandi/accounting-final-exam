🔹 Part 3: Case Study Analysis (15 Marks)

Case: GreenTech Solutions
GreenTech manufactures solar panels. They need to: evaluate new market profitability, provide audited statements to investors, undergo a tax review, implement real-time inventory software, and publish an ESG impact report.
  1. Which branch should evaluate new market profitability? Why? (2-3 sentences)
    ✅ Managerial/Cost Accounting – analyzes costs, revenues & break-even for strategic decisions.
  2. What statements for investors? Which branch ensures reliability?
    ✅ Financial Statements (Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Cash Flow); Auditing ensures reliability.
  3. Which branch handles tax review? Name TWO documents it prepares.
    ✅ Tax Accounting; documents: Tax Returns, Tax Provision/Compliance Reports.
  4. System/branch for real-time tracking? Give ONE benefit.
    ✅ AIS; benefit: real-time data, reduced errors, automated reporting.
  5. Which branch for environmental report? TWO non-financial metrics?
    ✅ Sustainability Accounting; metrics: CO₂ emissions reduced, % recycled materials used.

🔹 Part 4: Practical Problems (50 Marks)

Problem 1: Financial Accounting (5M)

Assets: $500k | Liabilities: $200k | Revenues: $350k | Expenses: $280k | Withdrawals: $30k. Calculate Ending Owner's Equity.

✅ Beg Equity = 500k-200k=300k | Net Income=350k-280k=70k | End Equity=300k+70k-30k=$340k
Problem 2: Managerial Accounting (5M)

Special order: 1,000 units @ $15 | VC/unit: $9 | FC: $50k (unaffected) | Normal price: $25. Accept?

✅ Rev: 15k | Cost: 9k | Profit: $6k → YES, accept (FC irrelevant).
Problem 3: Cost Accounting (5M)

DM: $40k | DL: $30k | MOH: $20k | Beg WIP: $5k | End WIP: $7k | Units: 5,000. Find COGM & Cost/Unit.

✅ Total Mfg=90k | COGM=5k+90k-7k=$88k | Cost/Unit=88k/5k=$17.60
Problem 4: Tax Accounting (5M)

Acct Profit: $200k | Non-deductible: $15k | Tax-exempt: $10k | Rate: 25%. Find Taxable Income & Tax Payable.

✅ Taxable=200k+15k-10k=$205k | Tax=205k×25%=$51,250
Problem 5: Auditing (5M)

Book Inv: $150k | Sample (10%): $15k | Errors: $450 overstatement. Materiality: $3k. Estimate total error & is it material?

✅ Error rate=3% | Total error=$4,500 | $4,500 > $3,000 → MATERIAL
Problem 6: Forensic Accounting (5M)

Expected daily sales: $2k | Actual deposited (5 days): $8.5k | Employee handled 3 days. Estimate skimmed amount & suggest 1 control.

✅ Expected=10k | Shortage=$1.5k | Control: Segregation of duties + pre-numbered receipts
Problem 7: Governmental Accounting (5M)

Beg Fund: $80k | Rev: $120k | Exp: $95k | Encumbrances: $15k. Calculate ending fund balance & why track encumbrances?

✅ End=80k+120k-95k-15k=$90k | Encumbrances prevent overspending appropriated budget
Problem 8: AIS (5M)

System: 1,200 invoices ($240k). Physical review: 5 invoices ($1k) issued but unrecorded. Error type? Prevention control?

✅ Completeness error | Control: Sequential numbering + gap detection + daily reconciliation
Problem 9: Sustainability (5M)

Profit: $500k | Env savings: $80k | Social inv: $45k | Carbon offsets: $15k. Calculate adjusted performance & 2 non-financial metrics.

✅ Adj=500k+80k-(45k+15k)=$520k | Metrics: CO₂ reduced, % renewable materials
Problem 10: Fiduciary Accounting (5M)

Beg Assets: $300k | Income: $18k | Expenses: $5k | Distribution: $50k. Prepare Statement of Changes in Fiduciary Net Position.

✅ End=300k+18k-5k-50k=$263k | Tracks assets held for others, not owned

🔹 Part 5: Critical Thinking (15 Marks)

  1. Compare Financial vs Managerial Accounting: Primary users, Time focus, Regulatory standards.
    ✅ Financial: External users, Past focus, GAAP/IFRS mandatory | Managerial: Internal users, Future focus, Flexible/No standards
  2. Why understand MULTIPLE branches? Give a practical example.
    ✅ Real problems are integrated. Example: Launching a product needs Cost (pricing), Managerial (feasibility), Tax (compliance), Financial (reporting).
  3. Advise a startup: Which 2 branches to prioritize first? Justify.
    ✅ Financial + Managerial/Cost. Startups need investor reporting (Financial) AND operational decision-making to survive/grow (Managerial).
